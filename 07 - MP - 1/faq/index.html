F.A.Q.

Equipment are designed to work at an allowed current. If equipment current exceeds the allowed current for a long duration, it may damage equipment and might also catch fire. In order to protect equipment from overload and short circuit, breakers are used which disconnects the equipment after a certain delay if any fault or overloading condition occurs.

For better reliability purpose it is important to properly calculate breaker size.
- Over size breaker will not trip under low fault or high overloading condition.
- Under size breaker will issue trip under normal condition.

It interrupts the flow of electrical current when it exceeds a predetermined amount, which is where a circuit breaker's ratings come into play.
